Traffic fines in Australia revealed information about a person's habits, age, and location. The most frequent infractions were obvious: using a cell phone while driving distracted drivers, failing to wear seat belts put lives at risk, speeding fines dominated the roads, and unlicensed driving slipped through the cracks. Young drivers, particularly those between the ages of 17 and 25, were at the top of the charts, and their carelessness or lack of experience cost them dearly.
